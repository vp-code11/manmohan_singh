About shree Manmohan singh

Dr. Singh, widely regarded for his intellect and vision, was sworn in as India’s Prime Minister on May 22, 2004, following the Congress Party’s victory in the general elections. He took office for a second term on the same date in 2009.

    Manmohan Singh, as both Finance Minister and Prime Minister of India, made several landmark decisions that significantly impacted the country's economic and social landscape. Here are some of the key decisions he took:
  • **Economic Liberalization (1991)**: - **Objective**: To open up the Indian economy and reduce government control over businesses. - **Impact**: Ended the License Raj, allowing private enterprises to operate freely, fostering industrial growth and job creation.
  • **Currency Devaluation (1991)**: - **Objective**: To boost trade and exports. - **Impact**: Devalued the Indian rupee by 20%, making Indian goods more competitive globally.
  • **Emergency Financial Measures (1991)**: - **Objective**: To address the balance-of-payments crisis. - **Impact**: Mortgaged gold reserves with the Bank of England to secure a critical loan from the International Monetary Fund (IMF), averting a national debt default.
  • > **Strengthening Financial Markets**: - **Objective**: To ensure transparency and trust in India's financial markets. - **Impact**: Empowered the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) as the sole regulator of stock markets and introduced tax concessions to support the services sector.
  • **Promoting Foreign Investment and Entrepreneurship**: - **Objective**: To boost industrialization and encourage domestic entrepreneurship. - **Impact**: Invited foreign direct investment and supported domestic businesses, accelerating India's transition to a globally competitive economy.
  • **Indo-US Nuclear Deal (2008)**: - **Objective**: To enhance energy security and international collaboration. - **Impact**: Secured the Indo-US nuclear deal, marking a turning point in India's energy policies despite political opposition.
  • **Implementation of MNREGA (2005)**: - **Objective**: To provide a safety net for rural households. - **Impact**: The Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MNREGA) provided guaranteed wage employment, enhancing rural livelihoods and ensuring financial inclusion.
  • **Right to Information Act (2005)**: - **Objective**: To strengthen transparency and accountability in governance. - **Impact**: Granted citizens the right to access information held by public authorities, promoting transparency.
  • **National Food Security Act (2013)**: - **Objective**: To ensure food security for the population. - **Impact**: Provided subsidized food grains to approximately two-thirds of India's population.
